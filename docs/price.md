# AlignEx Pricing

AlignEx pricing is designed for examination teams that need secure CBT delivery through online portal exams, offline center exams, or a combined delivery model. Offline delivery includes portal activation, exam package import, candidate delivery, answer capture, and result package export.

## Pricing Summary

| Plan | Price | Billing | Best For |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Free Plan | NGN 0 | Forever | Demonstration, testing, onboarding, and light practice use |
| Basic | NGN 50,000 | Yearly | Small users running simple online or offline traditional CBT exams |
| Standard | NGN 150,000 | Yearly | Schools, small CBT centers, and regular exam users |
| Professional | NGN 300,000 | Yearly | Professional schools, academies, NGOs, and certification bodies |
| Enterprise | NGN 750,000 | Yearly or contract | Large CBT centers, government agencies, recruitment bodies, and multi-location clients |

## Plan Details

### Free Plan

Free Plan is available forever for demonstrations, testing, onboarding, and light practice use. It is not recommended for official live exams.

- Price: NGN 0
- Duration: Forever
- Maximum candidates: 30
- Maximum exams per month: 2
- Maximum question banks: 1
- Maximum questions per bank: 100
- Maximum admin users: 1
- Maximum result exports per month: 2
- Maximum centers: 1
- Maximum devices: 1
- Official live exams: Not allowed
- Watermark: `DEMO MODE - NOT FOR OFFICIAL EXAM`

Included features:

- Traditional CBT
- Candidate import
- Question import
- CSV export
- Online exam delivery
- Offline activation
- Exam package import
- Result package export

Not included:

- PDF export
- Certificate generation
- Custom branding

## Notes For Implementation

- Free Plan should remain clearly marked as demo/practice-only and must not be used for official live exams.
- Paid plans should allow official live exams while respecting each plan limit.
- Plan checks should eventually be enforced during online exam delivery, offline activation, exam import, result export, device registration, and feature access.
- Enterprise limits should remain configurable because they may be contract-specific.
